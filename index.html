When is whale watching season in Hawaii?

Whale watching season runs December through April, with peak sightings in January-March. Thousands of humpback whales migrate from Alaska to Hawaii's warm waters to breed and give birth. Maui is particularly famous for whale watching, but you can see whales from all islands.

Do I need to know how to swim for snorkeling?

Basic swimming ability is recommended for most snorkeling tours. However, many operators provide flotation devices, and some tours offer shallow reef areas perfect for beginners. Always let your tour operator know your comfort level in advance.

What should I pack for Hawaii tours?

Essentials include reef-safe sunscreen (required by law), a hat, sunglasses, and comfortable water shoes. For water activities, bring a swimsuit, towel, and waterproof camera. Many tours provide snorkel gear. Bring a light jacket for helicopter tours or evening activities.

For popular activities like helicopter tours, luaus, and small-group snorkeling, book 2-4 weeks in advance, especially during peak season (December-April, June-August). Less popular tours can often be booked a few days ahead. Last-minute availability varies.

Can I see lava in Hawaii?

Active lava viewing depends on volcanic activity, which varies. Hawaii Volcanoes National Park on Big Island offers hiking, steam vents, and lava tube tours year-round. When Kilauea is actively erupting, special lava viewing tours become available. Check current conditions before your trip.

Tipping is customary and appreciated in Hawaii. For tours, 15-20% of the tour cost is standard for good service. For exceptional experiences, feel free to tip more. Tips go directly to your guides and crew, who often depend on gratuities as part of their income.
